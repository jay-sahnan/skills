# LinkedIn Hook Research (2025 Data)

The first 2-3 lines determine if anyone reads your post. Here's what the data shows.

## Hook Performance by Category

Research across 1M+ posts shows engagement rates by hook type:

| Hook Type | Engagement Rate | Comment Quality |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Contrarian Takes | 6.2% | Very High |
| Transformation Timeline | 5.3% | Very High |
| Pattern Interrupt | 5.1% | High |
| Problem-Solution Tension | 4.7% | High |
| Social Proof Stories | 4.2% | Very High |

## The Psychology That Works

Four neurological triggers that stop scrolling:

1. **Curiosity Gap** - Incomplete information triggers urgency to resolve
2. **Pattern Interrupt** - Violates expectations, activates attention
3. **Social Proof** - Relatable situations trigger mirror neurons
4. **Loss Aversion** - Fear of missing out > promise of gains

## Contrarian Hooks (6.2% engagement)

Challenge conventional wisdom with substance.

**Pattern**: [Common belief] is wrong. Here's why.

- "Most networking events are a waste of time."
- "Work-life balance is a myth."
- "Your resume doesn't matter as much as you think."

**Why it works**: Creates cognitive dissonance. Reader needs resolution.

## Transformation Timeline Hooks (5.3% engagement)

Show before/after with specific timeframe.

**Pattern**: [Time period] ago, I was [before state]. Now [after state].

- "2 years ago I was making $50K. This year I'll clear $300K."
- "6 months ago I couldn't get a single interview."
- "Last year our product had 10 users. Now we have 10,000."

**Why it works**: Specific timelines create credibility. Transformation creates hope.

## Pattern Interrupt Hooks (5.1% engagement)

Say something unexpected for professional context.

**Pattern**: [Unexpected statement that violates LinkedIn norms]

- "I got fired yesterday. Best thing that ever happened."
- "I turned down a $500K offer."
- "My biggest failure made me successful."

**Why it works**: Breaks scrolling pattern. Demands explanation.

## Problem-Solution Tension Hooks (4.7% engagement)

Set up a problem readers recognize.

**Pattern**: [Universal problem]. Here's what actually works.

- "Everyone tells you to network more. Nobody tells you how."
- "You're being productive but not making progress."
- "The advice you're getting is making things worse."

**Why it works**: Reader self-identifies with problem. Promise of solution.

## Hooks to Avoid (Algorithm Penalties)

LinkedIn's 2025 algorithm actively suppresses:

- "I'm excited to announce..." (corporate, skippable)
- "This one thing made me $X..." (scam ad pattern)
- "Stop scrolling, this is important" (demanding)
- "Unpopular opinion:" (usually popular opinion)
- Engagement bait ("Comment YES if...")
